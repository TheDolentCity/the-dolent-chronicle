I began playing tabletop roleplaying games in college and my first table always invested extra work into "Halloween" sessions. Sometimes silly, sometimes horrific, each year we found ourselves trying to outdo the previous experiences. When that campaign ended, we still committed to Halloween sessions with different game systems.

This year's Halloween session took place in March because we are all busy adults and two of us had to coordinate travel to New York City. The game: [**DREAD**](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dread_(role-playing_game)>). If you're unfamiliar, the core mechanic requires you to pull blocks from a Jenga Tower to overcome obstacles or avoid danger. If the tower falls, your character DIES (or is removed from the game). It's a compelling premise, even if the rules text itself is disappointing [(see this video essay for details)](https://youtu.be/apBz4IrjqBA?si=0BxYtX4-Rq7brmys). But the Game Master had a few ideas on how he wanted this game to be different from normal Dread.

## Fragmented

The GM titled the session _Fragmented_. Instead of each of us filling out a questionnaire to build a character backstory, we were building a backstory for another random player. Each player then began the game with no memory of who they were beyond their name and appearance.

As the game went on, we would regain our memories. Each one was a cut-out answer to a question from the questionnaire. I thought this would largely be a bit of a gimmick, but it turned out to be a big deal.

## The Session

This is the text that we were greeted with at the start of the game. I learned that I was **Sir Lars the Breaker**, an incredibly muscular man wearing exceedingly tight clothing. The rest of the cast included a manacled dwarf, cloaked woman, and a dark-haired woman.

As we set off, we came across some crystals. If the right character touched the crystal, it would dissolve and reveal an item, building, or person related to one of our memories. For me, this included finding a mace, my brother, and my beloved tailor's home.

The session was composed almost entirely of these trials and dramatic reveals, until we all had our memories back and arrived at the stomach. As we stood in the esophagus of this beast, we saw dozens and dozens of other openings. I concluded we were in a hydra, but I was only partially correct. A melodic tune was reverberating throughout the stomach, but we had no time to process this as hostile memory fragments attacked us. We smashed the remaining crystals that held this creature together with several pulls from the tower. It was on the verge of collapse, but we survived.

We escaped as the space collapsed around us, crawling out of the mouth of the beast. We then saw the beast in full as it writhed and died. Dozens of serpentine necks, each ending with a pale, human face. For the duration of the session, we had been trapped inside of a False Hydra.
